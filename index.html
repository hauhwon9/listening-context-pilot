Pilot Study

Listening Context and Music Similarity

Study Instructions

Thank you for taking part in this pilot study. The aim is to investigate whether listening context affects how people judge music similarity. Songs are used as familiar examples for subjective judgement; this is not a test of your musical knowledge.

There are no right or wrong answers. Please use your intuitive judgement of how similar the songs feel in the given listening situation. You may pause or take a short break at any point.

Scenario

1. Song Overview

This overview is optional. The same audio controls also appear inside each rating pair below.

2. Familiarity

Rate how familiar each song is to you.

3. Listen And Rate Each Pair

Listen to the two songs in each pair, then answer both directions below that pair. Treat Song A as the reference when answering A to B. Use 0 for not similar at all and 100 for extremely similar.

4. Feature Importance

How important were these features when judging similarity in this context? Use 1 for not important and 7 for very important.

5. Short Explanation

Briefly explain what made songs feel similar or different in this context.

Finish

Please download the JSON or CSV response file and send it back to the researcher. Your answers are saved only in this browser until you download them.
